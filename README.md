# Why Professional Reflection Matters

![Teacher Reflection](media/teacher_reflection.jpg)

Every teacher has experienced a lesson that did not go exactly as planned.

Perhaps students seemed disengaged, instructions were unclear, or the activity took longer than expected.

Professional growth begins with **reflection**—thinking critically about what happened, why it happened, and how future lessons can be improved.

Traditionally, teachers reflect by:
- Reviewing their lesson notes
- Discussing with colleagues or mentors
- Collecting student feedback

Today, AI can also support this process by asking reflective questions and suggesting possible improvements.

> **Remember:** AI should support your thinking—not replace your professional judgement.

# Prompt Engineering: Asking Better Questions

Good AI responses begin with good prompts.

A prompt is the instruction you give to an AI system. The more specific and purposeful your prompt is, the more useful the response is likely to be.

Let's compare two prompts.

---

## Prompt A

```text
Was my lesson good?
```

### Think About It

What information is missing?

- [( )] The teacher's name
- [(X)] The lesson context and specific details
- [( )] The classroom wall colour
- [( )] The school logo

> Correct!

> AI has very little information, so it can only give general advice.

---

## Prompt B

```text
Act as an experienced instructional coach.

I taught a 45-minute lesson on fractions to Grade 6 students.

Several students struggled during the group activity.

Ask me five reflective questions and suggest two practical ways to improve student engagement in my next lesson.

Keep your feedback constructive and specific.
```

---

## Compare the Two Prompts

| Prompt A | Prompt B |
|-----------|----------|
| Very short | Detailed and specific |
| No context | Includes lesson context |
| Generic answer | More personalised feedback |
| Difficult for AI | Easier for AI to understand |

---

## Knowledge Check

> Excellent!

> AI performs much better when the prompt clearly explains the situation, goal, and expected type of response.

## Example Expert Prompt

After writing your own prompt, compare it with this example.

```text
You are an experienced instructional coach.

I taught a 45-minute vocational lesson on workplace safety.

Several students became distracted during the practical activity, and some needed repeated instructions before completing the task.

Please:

• Ask me five reflective questions.
• Identify one strength in my lesson.
• Suggest three practical improvements for my next lesson.
• Explain why each suggestion may improve student engagement.

Keep your feedback supportive and constructive.
```

# Can You Trust AI's Feedback?

AI can generate useful suggestions, but **not every suggestion is appropriate**.

Professional teachers should always review AI-generated feedback before applying it in their classroom.

Let's look at an example.

---

## Example AI Response

Sarah asked AI for advice about her lesson.

The AI replied:

> "Your lesson could be improved by adding more student participation.
>
> You should:
>
> • Give clearer instructions before the practical activity.
>
> • Check students' understanding before moving on.
>
> • Break the practical task into smaller steps.
>
> • Encourage students to ask questions during the activity."

# Using AI Responsibly

![Ethical AI](media/ethics.jpg)

AI is a powerful professional learning tool, but it should always be used responsibly.

When using AI for professional reflection, remember these principles:

✅ Protect privacy by avoiding confidential student or colleague information.

✅ Verify AI suggestions before applying them in your teaching.

✅ Keep professional judgement at the centre of every decision.

Think of AI as a **thinking partner**, not a replacement for your expertise.
