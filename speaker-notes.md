# Speaker Notes
## AI as Your Professional Reflection Coach

**Duration:** 30 Minutes

---

# Section 1 – Welcome (2 minutes)

## What to Say

Good morning/afternoon everyone.

Today I'll be presenting my interactive LiaScript workshop titled **"AI as Your Professional Reflection Coach."**

This workshop is based on **one learning objective** from the UNESCO AI Competency Framework.

Instead of teaching many AI concepts, I focused on one specific competency:

> Helping teachers use AI to support professional self-reflection through effective prompt engineering.

Throughout this workshop participants will interact with quizzes, activities, a case study, videos, and a final challenge.

---

# Section 2 – Introduction

## What to Say

Before talking about AI, I first introduce a common challenge.

Every teacher finishes lessons wondering:

- What went well?
- What could be improved?
- How can I make the next lesson better?

Professional reflection is already part of good teaching.

The workshop introduces AI as a tool that can support this process—not replace it.

---

## Ask the Audience

"Have you ever used ChatGPT or another AI tool for learning or work?"

Allow a few responses before continuing.

---

# Section 3 – Reflection Activity

## What to Say

Participants answer a short quiz about professional reflection.

The purpose is to activate their prior knowledge before introducing AI.

This makes the learning experience more meaningful.

---

# Section 4 – AI as a Reflection Coach

## What to Say

Here I explain that AI can:

- ask reflective questions
- suggest improvements
- generate ideas

However,

AI only works well when we provide good prompts.

This naturally leads into prompt engineering.

---

# Pause Here

Ask:

"What happens if we ask AI a very vague question?"

Wait for answers.

Then continue.

---

# Section 5 – Prompt Engineering

## What to Say

This is the main learning activity.

Instead of giving participants a perfect prompt,

I first show a weak prompt.

They compare it with a stronger version and identify what makes it better.

The goal is to demonstrate that better prompts usually produce better AI responses.

---

# Explain

Mention that prompt engineering is an important professional skill because AI depends on clear instructions.

---

# Section 6 – Case Study

## What to Say

Participants now apply what they have learned.

They read Sarah's teaching scenario and write an AI prompt to help her reflect on her lesson.

Afterwards they compare their work with an expert example.

This is the main practical activity of the workshop.

---

# Section 7 – Evaluating AI Responses

## What to Say

Using AI is only one part of the process.

Teachers must also evaluate AI-generated feedback.

Participants review an AI response and decide which suggestions are useful.

This develops critical thinking instead of blind trust in AI.

---

# Section 8 – Responsible AI

## What to Say

This section reminds participants that teachers remain responsible for instructional decisions.

Three ideas are emphasized:

- Protect privacy.
- Verify AI suggestions.
- Use professional judgement.

AI supports teachers.

It does not replace them.

---

# Section 9 – Final Challenge

## What to Say

Participants now complete the final task.

They write their own AI prompt using everything they learned during the workshop.

This serves as the workshop's summative assessment because it directly measures the learning objective.

---

# Section 10 – Conclusion

## What to Say

To conclude, participants reflect on what they learned.

The workshop finishes with three key messages:

- Reflection improves teaching.
- Better prompts produce better AI responses.
- Teachers remain responsible for all professional decisions.

Finally, thank everyone for participating and invite questions.

---

# If My Teacher Asks Questions

## Why did you choose only one UNESCO objective?

The assignment required focusing on one learning objective.

Choosing a single objective allowed me to design every activity around one measurable competency instead of covering many topics superficially.

---

## Why this topic?

Professional reflection is something every teacher experiences.

AI can genuinely support this process, making the workshop practical and directly aligned with the UNESCO framework.

---

## Why prompt engineering?

Prompt engineering is the key skill that determines the quality of AI feedback.

Participants don't just learn about AI—they learn how to use it effectively.

---

## Where is Bloom's Taxonomy?

Bloom's Taxonomy is embedded naturally in the activities.

Participants:

- recall prior experiences,
- understand reflection,
- apply prompt engineering,
- analyse AI responses,
- evaluate AI suggestions,
- create their own prompt.

I intentionally avoided listing Bloom's levels because the assignment asked for them to be demonstrated naturally through learning activities.

---

## Why did you use LiaScript?

LiaScript allows interactive learning without additional software.

I used:

- quizzes
- images
- videos
- reflection activities
- case study
- self-assessment

These features make the workshop more engaging than a traditional presentation.

---

## Why is there both formative and summative assessment?

The quizzes throughout the workshop provide formative assessment by checking understanding and giving immediate feedback.

The final prompt-writing activity is the summative assessment because participants demonstrate the workshop's learning objective independently.

---

## Why didn't you let AI replace reflection?

Because the UNESCO framework emphasizes blending AI with human expertise.

The teacher remains responsible for evaluating AI suggestions and making professional decisions.
